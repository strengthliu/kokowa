FREQUENTLY ADKED QUESTIONS

Here are some questions and their answers, which are often mentioned. And maybe they are also your concern. We put these issues together and hope to help you.
  • What's WT1?

    The WT1 gene was isolated as the gene responsible for a childhood renal neoplasm, Wilms’ tumor, which was thought to arise as a result of inactivation of both alleles of the WT1 gene located at chromosome 11p1312. This gene encodes a zinc finger transcription factor that plays an important role in cell growth and differentiation 3 and its expression is restricted to a limited set of tissues, including the gonad, uterus, kidney and mesothelium, and to progenitor cells in various types of tissues 456. WT1 knock-out mice were found to have defects in the urogenital system and died on ED 13.5, probably due to heart failure 7.

  • What's KOKOWA®?

    KOKOWA® (WT1 tumor Vaccine, ) helps protect you against tumor with WT1-expressing. High expression of the WT1 gene in leukemias and solid tumors indicated that the WT1 protein might be a promising tumor-associated antigen (TAA). KOKOWA® may not fully protect everyone, nor will it protect against tumor not caused by WT1 gene. KOKOWA® does not prevent all types of cancer, so it’s important for you to continue routine cancer screenings. KOKOWA® does not treat cancer. KOKOWA® is a shot that is usually given in the armpit under the skin. KOKOWA® may be given as 2 or 3 shots. For persons 30 through 60 years of age, KOKOWA®is given using a 3-dose schedule; the second shot should be given 14days after the first shot and the third shot should be given 1 months after the first shot. The appropriate dosing schedule will be determined by you health care professional.

  • Who is suitable for the injection of WT1 vaccine?

    1. Patients who prevent recurrence after surgery;

    2. Tumor-susceptible population: including family history of cancer, positive tumor susceptibility gene detection, etc.;

    3. Bad living habits (smoking, alcohol abuse, bad eating habits), excessive stress during long-term work;

    Only a doctor or health care professional can decide if KOKOWA® is right for you.
