WT1 and cancer

The WT1 gene was isolated as the gene responsible for a childhood renal neoplasm, Wilms’ tumor, which was thought to arise as a result of inactivation of both alleles of the WT1 gene located at chromosome 11p13 (1,2). This gene encodes a zinc finger transcription factor that plays an important role in cell growth and differentiation (3) and its expression is restricted to a limited set of tissues, including the gonad, uterus, kidney and mesothelium, and to progenitor cells in various types of tissues (4–6). WT1 knock-out mice were found to have defects in the urogenital system and died on ED 13.5, probably due to heart failure (7).

What isKOKOWA®?

KOKOWA®（(WT1 Tumor Vaccine) helps protect you from tumors that are mutated by the WT1 gene. Due to the high expression of WT1 in hematological and solid tumors, we developed the WT1 tumor vaccine. Broad-spectrum tumor vaccine is mainly used for the prevention of tumors, leukemia, multiple myeloma, non-Hodgkin's lymphoma, lung cancer, breast cancer, stomach cancer, pancreatic cancer, liver cancer, cholangiocarcinoma, gallbladder cancer, colorectal cancer, kidney cancer , prostate cancer, ovarian cancer, thyroid cancer, melanoma, uterine body cancer, cervical cancer, glioma and other tumors have a preventive effect.
